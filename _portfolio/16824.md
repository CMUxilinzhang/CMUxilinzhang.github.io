## Introduction  
Contact-rich manipulation requires robots to reason not only about geometry, but also about physical interaction. Vision-only systems struggle under occlusion and cannot directly infer contact forces, while proprioceptive sensing alone lacks global spatial context. This limitation motivates multimodal approaches that fuse vision and proprioception for physically grounded perception.

**Objective:** Build a temporally aware, multimodal system that estimates **contact force and contact point sequences** directly from RGB images and proprioceptive signals, producing stable and interpretable contact representations for manipulation tasks.

---

## Methods  
Following a multimodal transformer-based pipeline:

- **Action Chunking with Transformer (ACT)** adapted from visuomotor policy learning to explicitly predict sequences of contact force and contact point instead of actions.  
<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/images/ACT.png" alt="Proprio-Phys teaser" width="800">
- **Multimodal fusion** of egocentric RGB images with joint positions, velocities, and joint torques, enabling the model to leverage both visual context and direct physical feedback.  
- **MuJoCo-based data collection** for contact-rich interactions, providing synchronized RGB, proprioception, and ground-truth contact supervision.

---

## Results  
- Larger transformer query sizes significantly improve temporal stability and physical consistency of predicted contact force and contact point trajectories.  
- Models with **Q = 80 and Q = 100** produce smooth, low-variance predictions that closely match ground truth, while small query sizes exhibit severe temporal jitter.  
- Training with larger query sets yields more stable convergence and improved generalization across contact conditions.

## Discussion  
The results show that contact estimation cannot be treated as a frame-by-frame regression problem. Instead, **long-horizon temporal modeling** is essential for capturing contact dynamics such as stick–slip transitions and sustained surface interaction.

By explicitly incorporating joint torque and leveraging transformer-based temporal reasoning, the model learns meaningful cross-modal associations that resolve ambiguities in vision-only perception. This makes the approach well suited for contact-rich manipulation and downstream control.
